### Q13. What does the principle of "absolute liability" imply in the context of the Bhopal gas tragedy?

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| a | Companies are liable only if negligence is proven |
| b | Companies have limited liability for any harm caused |
| **c ✅** | **Companies are fully liable for damages resulting from hazardous activities, regardless of precautions taken** |
| d | Liability is equally shared between the company and government agencies |

**✅ Correct Answer: c**

**Why c is correct:**
Slides quote the legal principle directly: *"The Principle of Absolute Liability states that when an enterprise is engaged in hazardous or inherently dangerous industry and if any harm results on account of such activity then the enterprise is absolutely liable to compensate for such harm and that it should be no answer to the enterprise to say that it had taken all reasonable care and that the harm occurred without any negligence on its part."*

**Why others are wrong:**
- **a** — This describes *strict liability* or traditional tort — requiring proof of negligence. Absolute liability goes further; negligence need not be proven.
- **b** — Limited liability is a corporate law concept protecting shareholders from personal liability — entirely different from environmental/industrial liability for victims.
- **d** — Under absolute liability, the enterprise bearing the hazardous activity carries *full* liability, not shared liability.

#### Type 1 — Wrong Option Spin-offs (Q13)

**Q13-T1a.** Which legal principle requires proof of *negligence* before a company can be held liable for damages — in contrast to absolute liability?
