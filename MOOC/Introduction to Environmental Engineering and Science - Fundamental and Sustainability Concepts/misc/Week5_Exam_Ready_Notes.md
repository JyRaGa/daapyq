### Q1. Why does a high BOD level in a receiving water body pose a threat to aquatic life?

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| a | It reduces carbon dioxide concentrations required for photosynthesis. |
| b | It causes excessive nutrient enrichment leading to algal blooms. |
| **c ✅** | **It depletes dissolved oxygen needed by aquatic organisms for survival.** |
| d | It increases the temperature of water, causing thermal pollution. |

**✅ Correct Answer: c**

**Why c is correct:**
BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand) measures the amount of oxygen consumed by microorganisms to decompose organic matter in water. The slides note that oxygen is consumed by unstable organic matter for oxidation; a high BOD means microbes rapidly deplete dissolved oxygen (DO), leaving insufficient O₂ for fish and other aquatic organisms to survive.

**Why others are wrong:**
- **a** — CO₂ is a *product* of decomposition, not depleted by BOD; photosynthesis is not the primary concern here.
- **b** — Nutrient enrichment causing algal blooms is *eutrophication*, driven by excess nitrogen/phosphorus, not directly by BOD.
- **d** — Thermal pollution is caused by heated discharges (e.g., power plant cooling water), not by BOD.

### Q7. A 2% solution of a sewage sample is incubated for 5 days at 20°C. The depletion of oxygen was found to be 4 ppm. Determine the BOD of the sewage.

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| a | 150 mg/L |
| b | 300 mg/L |
| c | 250 mg/L |
| **d ✅** | **200 mg/L** |

**✅ Correct Answer: d**

**Why d is correct:**

$$\text{BOD}_5 = \frac{DO_i - DO_f}{p}$$

Given:
- Dilution fraction $p = 2\% = 0.02$
- DO depletion $(DO_i - DO_f) = 4$ ppm

$$\text{BOD}_5 = \frac{4}{0.02} = 200 \text{ mg/L}$$

**Why others are wrong:**
- **a** — Would correspond to $4/0.0267$ — no valid dilution fraction gives this.
- **b** — Would correspond to $p = 4/300 \approx 1.33\%$ — not the given 2%.
- **c** — Would correspond to $p = 4/250 = 1.6\%$ — not the given 2%.

### Q9. 10 mL of wastewater sample is mixed with dilution water to make a total volume of 300 mL. It is desirable to have at least 1.5 mg/L DO drop during the 5-day test. What is the minimum detectable BOD₅ with this dilution?

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| a | 30 mg/L |
| b | 25 mg/L |
| **c ✅** | **45 mg/L** |
| d | 12 mg/L |

**✅ Correct Answer: c**

**Why c is correct:**

$$p = \frac{V_{sample}}{V_{total}} = \frac{10}{300} = \frac{1}{30}$$

$$\text{Minimum BOD}_5 = \frac{DO_i - DO_f}{p} = \frac{1.5}{1/30} = 1.5 \times 30 = 45 \text{ mg/L}$$

**Why others are wrong:**
- **a** — 30 mg/L would require $p = 1.5/30 = 0.05$, i.e., a 5% dilution — not the case here.
- **b** — 25 mg/L would require $p = 0.06$ — not the case here.
- **d** — 12 mg/L would require $p = 0.125$ — not the case here.

### Q10. Which parameter is used to express the theoretical oxygen demand (ThOD) of organic matter in water?

| Option | Text |
|--------|------|
| a | Dissolved oxygen (DO) |
| b | Chemical oxygen demand (COD) |
| c | Biochemical oxygen demand (BOD) |
| **d ✅** | **Oxygen equivalent based on stoichiometry** |

**✅ Correct Answer: d**

**Why d is correct:**
ThOD is calculated by writing the complete oxidation reaction for the organic compound and computing oxygen needed based on stoichiometry. It is a theoretical value, not measured by biological (BOD) or chemical (COD) tests.

**Why others are wrong:**
- **a** — DO measures *available* dissolved oxygen, not demand.
- **b** — COD measures oxygen demand using *chemical* oxidation (potassium dichromate), not stoichiometry alone.
- **c** — BOD measures oxygen consumed by *microbial* activity over 5 days.
