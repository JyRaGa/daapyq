## 💧 SECTION 2: THE CAPE TOWN WATER CRISIS (Running on Empty)

**1. "Day Zero":** This does NOT just mean a "water shortage." Day Zero is the apocalyptic date when the city officially **shuts down the municipal piped water supply**, forcing citizens to line up at public taps for daily rations.

**2. The Mediterranean Climate Trap:** Cape Town has a Mediterranean climate — it gets its rain in **Winter (May–August)** and is completely dry in Summer. Three consecutive dry winters (2015–2018) caused the crisis.

**3. Who Uses the Water? (The 70/30 Rule):**

- **70%** → Urban and Industrial use
- **30%** → Agriculture
- *(MCQ Trap: Do NOT reverse these! Agriculture uses less than the city.)*

**4. Water Demand Management (WDM) — Specific Initiatives (All of the Above MCQ):**

Cape Town used all four of these simultaneously:
1. **Restrictions** (mandatory usage limits)
2. **Tariff hikes** (making water expensive to deter overuse)
3. **Pressure management** (reducing pipe pressure to stop bursting and reduce flow)
4. **Smart meters** in schools
5. **Behavioral nudges** — the "Cape Town Water Map" publicly shamed water wasters

- **Unit Conversion:** **1 Gallon = 3.785 Litres** (often rounded to **3.7 L** in MCQs).

## 🗑️ SECTION 3: PANAJI'S GOLDEN SWM RULE (The 5 Bins)

Panaji achieved a zero-landfill, 100% door-to-door waste collection system through aggressive source segregation. You MUST know the 5 bin colors and their collection frequencies:

| Bin Colour | Waste Type | Collection Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 **Green** | Wet waste (food/organic) | **Daily** |

## ☠️ SECTION 4: THE BHOPAL GAS TRAGEDY (The Nightmare)

**5. The Chemistry:**

On **December 3, 1984**, water entered a storage tank at the Union Carbide plant. This caused a massive exothermic reaction, releasing approximately **40 tons of Methyl Isocyanate (MIC)** gas over the city.

**Chemical Sequence:**

```
Methylamine + Phosgene → MIC + 1-Naphthol → Carbaryl (Sevin)
```

- **The final pesticide product** being manufactured was **Carbaryl**, marketed as **Sevin**.
- **Prime Accused CEO:** Warren Anderson (Union Carbide Corporation).

**6. Absolute Liability:**

The legal principle established in India after this disaster. A factory dealing with hazardous materials has **absolutely NO excuse** if something goes wrong — they must pay damages regardless of negligence. No exceptions.

## 🍎 SECTION 5: FOOD WASTE & REVOLUTIONARY TECH

**7. SecondBite (Australia):**

An organization tackling food waste through **redistribution** — not composting or recycling. They rescue surplus food from farmers and supermarkets and give it directly to community groups.

**8. Hydrothermal Carbonization (HTC — "Zero Loss"):**

Developed by **IIT Kharagpur**, HTC converts wet biomass directly into fuel.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Input | Wet biomass (no drying needed) |
| Output | **Hydrochar** (Bio-coal) |
| Temperature | **180–250°C** |
| Key advantage | **NO drying step** required — beats incineration on energy efficiency |

**9. Global Food Waste Framework:**

- **Highest Loss Region:** **Industrialized Asia** has the highest share (**28%**) of global food loss and waste.
- **Target-Measure-Act Framework:** The **"Measure"** step is critical — it identifies *where* and *how much* food is being lost before action can be taken.

**10. Virtual Water:**

The volume of freshwater used to produce a product, measured at the place where the product was actually produced.

| Product | Virtual Water Footprint |
|---|---|
| 1 cup of coffee | 130 L (lowest) |
| 1 kg of sugar | 1,800 L |
| 1 kg of beef | ~15,000 L (highest) |

**Feynman Challenge:** Explain the "Absolute Liability" principle established after Bhopal.

> **Example Answer:** Normally, if a factory has an accident, they can argue it wasn't their fault (e.g., "it was a natural disaster" or "a worker made a mistake"). **Absolute Liability** says: If you are doing something inherently dangerous (like making toxic MIC gas) and it leaks, you are **100% responsible** for all damage, period. No excuses, no exceptions.
