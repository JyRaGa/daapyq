#### Part 1: Sustainability & Frameworks
* **1. Sustainability (The Brundtland Definition):** Meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their needs. 
* **2. The Triple Bottom Line:** 

![Image of Triple Bottom Line Venn diagram](https://encrypted-tbn0.gstatic.com/licensed-image?q=tbn:ANd9GcQyzCdgIP-QmUPq-gtNvDUS8mKbJyXT9P6I1XTKBVbiKYOT36whukm_7pLT53O8m2RngNwSbRnFRDurEVmgkWRrMhbMaCnFAiDB2yq_5YZBpZCLFuM)
 A framework integrating three pillars: **Planet** (Environment), **People** (Society), and **Profit** (Economy).
    * **🔥 Walkthrough Trap (The Intersections):** * Society + Environment = **Bearable**
        * Economy + Environment = **Viable**
        * Society + Economy = **Equitable**
* **3. The IPAT Equation:**  Environmental **Impact (I)** = **Population (P)** × **Affluence (A)** × **Technology (T)**. 
	* **Urbanization & Demographics (2019/2023 Facts):**
    * Almost all population growth is expected in cities and towns of **developing (low and middle-income)** countries. High-income countries have plateaued.
    * Rapid, unplanned urbanization is guaranteed to cause significant **land-use changes** (converting agriculture/forest to infrastructure).
* **Water Crisis (2019 Fact):** 80% of diseases in developing countries are due to a lack of access to clean potable water. The most effective management intervention is providing **point-of-use water purification systems** and promoting hygiene (bypassing contaminated municipal piping).
* **Sustainability Frameworks (2019 Fact):** To attain sustainability goals, environmental engineers rely primarily on two combined frameworks: **Systems Thinking** and **Life Cycle Thinking**.
* **4. Circular Economy:** 

![Image of Circular Economy concept](https://encrypted-tbn1.gstatic.com/licensed-image?q=tbn:ANd9GcSuL1X4yVDjkBpPRx3wJ6x5W8ZXuqZsZfqWc0J48Ax8xwnmhoi--H2Yvu486FW8fNoNWYR6hDr5UHT1izpCqjWIrQYiTKgB41hhs0J6npEnLLSzQEo)
Moving away from "take-make-dispose" to a closed-loop system of **Make - Use - Return**. It keeps materials in the economy without leaking into the environment.
* **5. Tragedy of the Commons:** When individuals act rationally in their own self-interest but end up depleting a shared resource, harming everyone (e.g., overfishing, air pollution).
* **6. Ecological Footprint:** Measures the land/water required to sustain a population. 
    * *Spin-off Fact:* Vancouver's footprint is **19 times** its own area. Globally, humanity consumes **1.7 Earths'** worth of resources.
* **7. Earth Overshoot Day:** The date when humanity's demand for ecological resources in a given year exceeds what Earth can regenerate in that year.
* **8. Carrying Capacity & Limits to Growth:**  Populations grow *geometrically* (multiplying), while resources grow *arithmetically* (adding). If population crosses the resource line, we hit the carrying capacity, causing a crisis.

#### Part 2: Cities, Water, and Resources
* **9. Urbanization:** Urban areas cover only **~1% of land** but produce **60–70% of anthropogenic GHG emissions**. 
    * *Spin-off Facts:* Urbanization in high-income countries is plateauing; almost all future growth is in **low/middle-income developing countries**. Urban growth by 2050 will require **100–200 million extra hectares** of land.
    * *Timeline:* 2 out of 10 people lived in cities in 1900 $\rightarrow$ 5 out of 10 in 2010 $\rightarrow$ projected **7 out of 10 by 2050**.
    * A city with > 10 million people is a **Megacity / Urban city**.
* **10. Daily Resource Use:** The average person consumes **>50 kg** of resources per day.
* **11. Water and Disease:** Lack of clean water causes **80% of diseases** in developing countries. 
    * *Spin-off Facts:* **98%** of water-related deaths occur in the developing world. **84%** of these involve children aged 0–14. A child dies every **15 seconds** from dirty water. Dirty water kills **over 5 million people** a year (more than all violence/war).

#### Part 3: Engineering, LCA & Green Chemistry
* **12. The Design Stage:** The engineering design stage offers the **maximum degrees of freedom** for sustainable choices. About 80% of environmental impact is locked in here.
* **13. Three Levels of Sustainable Design:** 
    * *Level 1: Optimize* (Incremental improvements, like a more efficient gas engine).
    * *Level 2: Reengineer* (Changing the technology, like a hybrid/electric car).
    * *Level 3: Redefine the Problem* (Changing the system, like building walkable transit cities instead of cars).
* **14. Life Cycle Assessment (LCA):** Cradle-to-grave analysis. 
    * *Spin-off Facts:* For automobiles, the **use stage** has the highest environmental impact (fuel, road runoff). For buildings, the **operation/occupancy stage** consumes the most energy/water.
* **15. Green Chemistry Principles:**
    * *Principle 1:* **Prevent waste** (better to prevent than treat).
    * *Principle 2:* **Atom Economy** (maximize incorporation of all starting materials into the final product).
    * *Principle 8:* **Reduce derivatives** (avoid protecting groups to reduce extra waste).
    * *Principle 11:* **Real-time analysis** for pollution prevention.
* **16. The E-Factor (Exact Formula Sheet Match):** Calculate the E-factor for a chemical production process to measure material efficiency and waste generation.
    * **Formula:** `E-factor = Total Mass of Waste / Total Mass of Product`
    * **Note:** `Total Mass of Waste = Total Mass of Inputs - Total Mass of Desired Product`
    * **Units Required:** Mass of Waste: kg (or grams) | Mass of Product: kg (or grams) | E-factor: dimensionless (kg waste / kg product). A lower E-factor means a greener process.
* **17. The CFL Lightbulb Trade-off:** CFLs save energy but contain toxic **Mercury (Hg)** (around 4 mg). Without system thinking, we miss this trade-off.
* **18. Jevons Paradox:** Increased technology efficiency lowers costs, which can lead to *overuse* of finite resources.

#### Part 4: History, Laws & Global Agreements
* **1962 - Silent Spring:** Rachel Carson's book exposed pesticide (DDT) impacts, jumpstarting the modern environmental movement.
* **1969 - The Cuyahoga River Fire:** Industrial pollution caused a river in Ohio to literally catch fire. *This directly led to the creation of the EPA and the US Clean Water Act (Tested in 2025).*
* **19. Key Disasters & Tragedies:**
    * **Cuyahoga River Fire (1969):** Led to the **Clean Water Act** and the creation of the EPA.
    * **Love Canal:** Toxic chemical dump under a neighborhood; became the first **Superfund** site leading to **CERCLA (1980)**.
    * **Silent Spring (1962):** Book by Rachel Carson that led to the banning of **DDT** pesticides.
    * **Donora Smog (1948):** Deadly smoke cloud leading to the **Clean Air Act**.
    * **Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984):** Union Carbide leaked **40 tons** of **Methyl Isocyanate (MIC)**. 
        * *🔥 Slide Veto Trap:* Methyl Isocyanate is **-NCO**. Do NOT confuse it with Methyl Isocyanide (**-NC**), which is a different functional group. The plant was making the pesticide Carbaryl (Sevin).
* **20. The RCRA (1976):** Resource Conservation and Recovery Act. Manages hazardous waste from generation to transport, treatment, storage, and disposal ("cradle-to-grave").
* **21. Policy Types:** Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) is a **Regulation** (forced), while Eco-labeling is a **Voluntary Program**.
* **22. Global Agreements:**
    * **Montreal Protocol (1987):** Addressed **Ozone-depleting substances**.
    * **Earth Summit (1992):** Rio de Janeiro. Set *non-binding* goals to return CO₂ to 1990 levels.
    * **Kyoto Protocol (1997):** Mandated *binding* cuts of CO₂ by **6 to 8%** from 1990 levels (by 2008-2012).
    * **Paris Agreement (2016):** Replaced the **8** Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) with the **17** Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
* **23. The SDGs:** SDG 2 = Zero Hunger; SDG 5 = Gender Equality; **SDG 6 = Clean Water & Sanitation**; SDG 13 = Climate Action.

## 🚀 ACTIVE RECALL & FEYNMAN CHALLENGE
*Cover the answers below and test yourself before the exam.*

**Quiz 1:** A chemical reaction uses 20 kg of inputs and produces 4 kg of the desired product. Calculate the E-factor.
> **Answer:** Waste = 20 kg - 4 kg = 16 kg. E-factor = 16 / 4 = **4.0**. (For every 1 kg of product, 4 kg of waste is produced).

**Quiz 2:** True or False: "Efficiency improvements in technology alone are sufficient to achieve sustainability, as long as population stays constant."
> **Answer:** **False.** The Jevons Paradox proves that as tech becomes more efficient, it becomes cheaper, leading to an increase in overall consumption (Affluence in the IPAT equation goes up, negating the Technology efficiency).

**Feynman Challenge:** Explain the *Tragedy of the Commons* as if you were talking to a 10-year-old.
> **Example Answer:** Imagine a jar of cookies left in the kitchen for the whole family to share over a week. If you act selfishly and take three cookies today because they taste good (your rational self-interest), and your siblings do the same, the jar is empty by Tuesday. Because nobody owned the jar individually, nobody protected it, and now everyone suffers with no cookies for the rest of the week.
